GSHL Rulebook
Rosters
1.1 - Each teams roster will consist of 2 Centers, 2 Left Wingers, 2 Right Wingers, 3 Defensemen, 1 Utility Skater, 1 Goalie, and 4 Bench Spots.
1.2 - A maximum of two healthy goalies is allowed on a roster at any point in time.
(i) Violators of this rule will have one of their goalies dropped at random by the commissioner.
1.3 - Teams will have 1 IR slot and 1 IR+ slot for injured players.

Salary Cap/Salaries
7.1 - The Salary Cap will be a hard cap of $22,500,000. This number is not going to fluctuate year-to-year and may not be exceeded at any point in time.
7.2 - Each player will be given a salary at the start of every signing period based on the algoritm. Salaries will range between $10,000,000 and $1,000,000.
7.3 - There will be two signing periods and one free agency period every year.
(i) The mid-season signing period will start on December 15th and end on December 31st.
(ii) The end of year signing period will begin at the end of the GSHL playoffs and run until the end of the NHL playoffs.
(iii) The free agency period will run from the day after the Stanley Cup is awarded until the day of the GSHL draft.
7.4 - A player's salary will be locked in when the contract is signed, not when the contract begins. This salary will not change for the length of the contract.
Contracts/Signings
8.1 - There is a 3-year maximum on all contracts. Contracts can only be 1, 2, or 3 years in length.
8.2 - Each team is allowed to carry a maximum of three contracts at once.
8.3 - Contracts can be signed at any point during a signing period by messaging the commissioner or the group.
(i) Your message must include the player you are signing and the number of years you are signing him for.
(ii) If a team signs a contract that puts them over the cap, the contract will not be allowed and the team will lose 200 draft lottery points.
8.4 - Contracts signed during the season will not officially begin until the end of that season. Contracts will start at the end of the GSHL playoffs, or the date of signing if later, and finish at the end of the GSHL playoffs.
8.5 - Players that are in the final year of their first contract can be resigned for 110% of their posted salary.
8.6 - If a player under contract retires or leaves the NHL for a European pro league, their contract will be voided immediately with no penalty.
8.7 - Contracts can only be terminated with a buyout. If a player under contract is dropped from their team's roster this will immediately be considered a buyout.
(i) Buyouts will not use up a contract slot but will come with a cap hit of two thirds the players salary for the same length of the contract.
(i) If the player is in the final year of their contract the buyout will be a cap hit of two thirds the players salary for the remainder of that final year and one half of the players salary for the following season.
Free Agency
9.1 - The moment that the Stanley Cup is awarded to its winner, every player that does not have a contract will become a free agent.
9.2 - Players that have just finished their second consecutive contract can not be signed as a free agent and must enter the draft.
9.3 - At the start of free agency every player will be given a new salary equal to 120% of their posted salary.
9.4 - Teams can send in a free agent proposal at any time prior to the draft. Proposals will be processed every Sunday.
(i) If there is only one proposal for a player, then the player will sign that contract.
(ii) If there is more than one proposal for a player, then there will be a lottery to determine which contract the player will sign.
9.5 - The free agent lottery will use the current draft lottery points of all the teams that made a proposal to determine the odds of the free agent lottery
