GSHL Rulebook

Rosters
1.1 - Each teams roster will consist of 2 Centers, 2 Left Wingers, 2 Right Wingers, 3 Defensemen, 1 Utility Skater, 1 Goalie, and 4 Bench Spots.
1.2 - A maximum of two healthy goalies is allowed on a roster at any point in time.
(i) Violators of this rule will have one of their goalies dropped at random by the commissioner.
1.3 - Teams will have 1 IR slot and 1 IR+ slot for injured players.
Scoring/Categories
2.1 - Scoring will be done by stat categories, each worth one point. There will be 7 skater categories and 3 goalie categories for a total of 10 stat categories.
(i) The 7 skater categories will be Goals, Assists, Points, Powerplay Points, Shots, Hits, and Blocks.
(i) The 3 goalie categories will be Wins, Goals Against Average, and Save Percentage.
2.2 - There will be an additional half-point category that will be awarded to the home team in each matchup. This will be called the "home-ice advantage".
2.3 - Teams must have a minimum of 2 goalie starts during a matchup. If a team does not meet this minimum, then they will concede all 3 goalie cateogries.
Waivers/Trades
3.1 - Any player dropped from a roster will remain on waivers for 2 days until they are processed. During this time teams can place waiver claims on the player.
3.2 - Waivers will be processed using Yahoo's continuous rolling list system.
3.3 - If a player is not claimed they will become a free agent.
3.4 - Trades can be made at any time and are not subject to any league or commissioner approval.
(i) Any trades with suspected foul play or collusion will be investigated immediately and violaters will be punished at commissioners discretion
3.5 - Players under contract are allowed to be traded. Their contract will travel with them to their new team intact and the new team is responsible for making sure this contract does not exceed any contract or salary cap limits
(i) If a team receives a contract via trade that puts them over the salary cap or contract limit they will be penalized 100 lottery points and the trade will be reversed immediately
(ii) If a team trades a contract to a team and that trade becomes ineligible then they will also be penalized 50 lottery points. Teams that are trading away a player under contract are responsible for making that players contract situation known to all parties.
(iii) The commissioner reserves the right to adjust to above punishments if circumstances require.
Schedule/Tiebreakers
4.1 - The GSHL season will be the length of the NHL regular season minus 3 playoff weeks. (Usually 21 weeks, sometimes 22)
4.2 - Each team will play a home-and-home with every team in their conference for a 14 game conference schedule. The rest of the schedule will be non-conference games.
(i) Crossover non-conference games will rotate the home team every other year.
(ii) If the non-conference schedule is only 7 games, one crossover matchup will be skipped based on an 8-year rotation.
4.3 - If teams in the same conference are tied in the standings, the tiebreaker will be as follows. (If there are more than two teams tied, once a team is eliminated, the process restarts)
(i) Head-to-Head between record between the teams
(ii) Conference records
(iii) Record vs 1st in conference, then 2nd in conference, etc.
(iv) Higher Point Differential
(v) Higher Power Ranking
4.4 - If teams in different conferences are tied in the standings, the tiebreaker will be as follows. (If there are more than two teams tied, once a team is eliminated, the process restarts)
(i) Head-to-Head between record between the teams
(ii) Non-Conference records
(iii) Higher Point Differential
(iv) Higher Power Ranking
Playoffs/Payouts
5.1 - The top 3 teams in each conference qualify for the playoffs along with the best two remaining teams as wildcards.
(i) If the two wildcard teams are from different conferences then there will be no crossover and the matchups will be 1st v 4th and 2nd v 3rd in each conference
(ii) If the two wildcard teams are from the same conference then the better wildcard team will play the worse conference winner and the worse wildcard team will play the better conference winner while 2nd v 3rd play in each conference.
5.2 - Second round playoff matchups will be considered Conference Championship games.
5.3 - The GSHL Cup Final will match up the two conference champions.
5.4 - When a team is eliminated from the playoffs their roster is locked immediately and there are no consolation games.
5.5 - The yearly buy-in for each team is $50. Yearly Payouts are $600 for the GSHL Cup champion, $150 for the GSHL Cup runner up, and the final $50 go to admin fees (engraving, etc.).
Draft/Draft Lottery
6.1 - The GSHL Draft will be held within 3 weeks of the start of the NHL season.
6.2 - The Draft will be 12 rounds long and follow a snake draft format.
(i) Teams without three players under contract at draft time will have to fill their remaining roster spots via waivers.
6.3 - The draft order will be decided by a draft lottery. The lottery odds will be done by a points system.
(i) Teams will be given a base point total based on their regular season and playoff finish.
(ii) Teams can then be awarded extra lottery points by winning player/team awards. (And more to come)
(iii) The commissioner can also award lottery points to a team at his discretion based on engagement/effort
Salary Cap/Salaries
7.1 - The Salary Cap will only apply to each teams keepers from year-to-year. The majority of a team's roster will not be part of the salary cap system. Team's will 'keep' players based on the contract system outlined below.
7.2 - The Salary Cap will have a hard ceiling of $22,500,000. This number is not going to fluctuate year-to-year and may not be exceeded at any point in time.
7.3 - Each player will be given a salary at the start of every signing period based on the algoritm. Salaries will range between $10,000,000 and $1,000,000.
7.4 - There will be two signing periods and one free agency period every year.
(i) The mid-season signing period will start on December 15th and end on December 31st.
(ii) The post-season signing period will begin at the end of the GSHL playoffs and run until the end of the NHL playoffs.
(iii) The free agency period will run from the day after the Stanley Cup is awarded until the day of the GSHL draft.
7.5 - A player's salary will be locked in when the contract is signed, not when the contract begins. This salary will not change for the length of the contract.
Contracts/Signings
8.1 - There is a 3-year maximum on all contracts. Contracts can only be 1, 2, or 3 years in length.
8.2 - Each team is allowed to carry a maximum of three contracts at once. The rest of the roster will be filled with non-contract players who will cycle through the draft.
(i) Teams do not have to sign three contracts. There is no minimum contract amount.
(ii) Players must be on your team's current roster and have been on your team's roster for a minimum of 11 weeks during the season, or any GSHL roster for a minimum of 17 weeks, to be eligible to sign a contract with your team.
8.3 - Contracts can be signed at any point during a signing period by messaging the commissioner or the group.
(i) Your message must include the player you are signing and the number of years you are signing him for.
(ii) If a team signs a contract that puts them over the cap, the contract will not be allowed and the team will lose 200 draft lottery points.
8.4 - Contracts signed during the mid-season signing period will not officially begin until the end of that season. Contracts signed during the post-season signing period or free agency period will start immediately.
8.5 - Contract years will finish at the end of the GSHL playoffs. Players under contract will have a year come off their contracts at this point.
8.6 - Players that are in the final year of their first contract can be resigned during the post-season signing period for 115% of their posted salary.
8.7 - If a player under contract retires or leaves the NHL for a European pro league, their contract will be voided immediately with no penalty.
8.8 - Contracts can only be terminated with a buyout. If a player under contract is dropped from their team's roster this will immediately be considered a buyout.
(i) Buyouts will not use up a contract slot but will come with a cap hit of half the players salary for two years.
Free Agency
9.1 - The moment that the Stanley Cup is awarded to its winner, every player that does not have a contract will become a free agent.
9.2 - Players that have just finished their second consecutive contract can not be signed as a free agent and must enter the draft.
9.3 - At the start of free agency every player will be given a new salary equal to 125% of their posted salary.
9.4 - Teams can send in a free agent proposal at any time prior to the draft. Proposals will be processed on the first Sunday of every month.
(i) If there is only one proposal for a player, then the player will sign that contract.
(ii) If there is more than one proposal for a player, then there will be a lottery to determine which contract the player will sign.
9.5 - The free agent lottery will use the current draft lottery points of all the teams that made a proposal to determine the odds of the free agent lottery
